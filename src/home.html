Al-Atheena presented a working paper on regional connectivity of the Internet

  • 30th Nov 2018

The head of the Communications and Information Technology Regulatory Authority, Salem Al-Atheena, on Thursday stressed the importance attached by Kuwait to the regional network connectivity project. This came in a statement to KUNA at the end of his participation as head of the Kuwaiti delegation in the 22nd session of the Council of Arab Ministers of Communications and Information at the headquarters of the League of Arab States. In this context, Al-Atheena explained that Kuwait presented to the meeting a working paper on the regional network connectivity project which is scheduled to be discussed during the next meeting of the Arab Network Committee. He pointed out that the meeting discussed several issues through the fields of mail and communications, pointing out that there are several recommendations, most importantly the adoption of the results of the World Conference of Delegates Plenipotentiary held in the UAE. He pointed out that the meeting touched on many topics in the forefront of modern technology, Internet technology, artificial intelligence and so-called "portfolios" and the possibility of Arab countries entering this technology. He also noted the Arab efforts to unite efforts and expertise during the coming period in order to benefit peoples, facilitate procedures and make the life of Arab societies easier and better. Al-Atheena said that during its participation in the Executive Office of the Arab Ministers of Communications and Information Council in the previous session over the past two years, Kuwait has had multiple participations through proposals and working papers.

Chairman of the Telecommunications and Information Technology Regulatory Authority Salem Al-Atheena highlighted the importance Kuwait has attached to the regional network interconnection project.

  • 29th Nov 2018

This came in a statement to Kuwait News Agency (KUNA) at the end of his participation as head of the Kuwaiti delegation in the 22nd session of the Arab Council of Ministers of Communications and Information at the headquarters of the League of Arab States. In this context, Al-Atheena stated that Kuwait had submitted to the meeting a working paper on the regional interconnection project for internet networks, which is scheduled to be discussed during the next meeting of the Arab Network Committee. He pointed out that the meeting discussed several issues in the field of postal and communications, pointing out that there are several recommendations, including the adoption of the results of the World Conference of Delegates authorized in the UAE. He pointed out that the meeting touched on many topics in the forefront of modern technologies, Internet technology, artificial intelligence and so-called "portfolios" and the possibility of Arab countries entering this technology. He also noted the Arab action to unify efforts and experiences during the next phase in order to benefit the peoples and facilitate the procedures and make the life of the Arab communities better and better. Al-Ateena pointed out that Kuwait, during its participation in the Executive Office of the Arab Ministers of Communications and Information Council during the previous two-year session, had several participations through proposals and working papers. He also pointed to the selection of a new executive office for the Council today, according to the relevant regulations, and through the nomination of names according to the alphabetical letters. The delegation of Kuwait included the head of coordination and follow-up to the office of the chairman of the Commission, Badr Al-Sawag, the director of the infrastructure administration Khalid Al-Azmi and the director of international organizations and research, Youssef Al-Zafairi.
